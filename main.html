Who we serve

Our Staff and Volunteers focus on serving the foreign-born population in Richmond, Virginia. While some of our Community Partners may have specific requirements for income limitations and/or restrictions, length of residency and visa status, here at City Oasis we do not. We are here for you. So no matter if it is your first day here in Richmond, or you have been here for 20 years and are now acclimated and an established business owner, we may be able to help connect you to to additional local resources, or even as a volunteer to help others .

Immigrant

A person who comes with the intent to live permanently in a foreign country. We also have resources to assist in helping undocumented peoples become documented and legal citizens.

International Student

A person who has left his or her country, or territory of origin, and moved to another country or territory with the singular objective of studying.

Refugee

Someone who has been forced to flee his or her country because of persecution, war, or violence. They have done this because of a well-founded fear of persecution for reasons of race, religion, nationality, political opinion or membership in a particular social group

Asylum Seeker

A person who says he or she is a refugee, but whose claim has not yet been definitively evaluated.

Resources Provided

We help by providing resources for educational, cultural, medical, legal, and practical assistance. So if you need help with learning English, taking your citizenship test, legal services, or just finding local ethnic markets, let us know. We can help!

  • English as a Second Language (ESL)

    We can help connect you with the many established ESL and TEOFL classes. Whether you are looking for an official certificate level program, or just conversation practice, we can help.

  • Citizenship Test

    Are you preparing for your citizenship test? Do you need someone to study with? Our volunteers would love to help!

Volunteer Opportunities

Looking for a place to volunteer? Our volunteers play a vital role in the work of welcoming, acclimating and empowering the foreign-born population of Richmond, Virginia. Many of our volunteers choose to do so as a public service, for civil and humanitarian as well as personal development reasons. These opportunities will serve as ways to connect culturally curious volunteers with those from the foreign-born population for a mutually beneficial education and experience. Some of our volunteers have also received college credits!

We can connect you with many different opportunities to serve with your time, gifts, and talents. Through a personal interview, and the volunteer application process, we introduce volunteers to various options for service. Whatever the unique schedule, context preference, and areas of interest, we strive to create a comfortable fit for both international and volunteer.

  • Convenient to your schedule

    We will work to see if we can connect you in ways that will fit your available hours and desired commitment duration. We currently have daily, weekly, monthly and seasonal opportunities.

  • Diverse volunteering options

    We know that each of our volunteers have different talents. So, we offer many different options to better fit for your passions and abilities. We want our volunteers to love what they do!

  • Do you need Volunteers?

    Are one of our community partners or resources that is available to our clients? Would you like to let others know that they can volunteer with your program? We would love to connect you with our approved volunteers!

  • More info

    If you would like one of our information packets on volunteering, or would like help with our volunteer application, please email us at info@cityoasis.org
